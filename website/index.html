A DevOps course for the community

Limited slots available for this course on February, 2020. The registration form is an expression of interest form only, and does not guarantee participation in class.

This open source course is aimed to help those professionals from infrastructure or development that want to learn and practice DevOps practices and culture.

This course is both introductory and fast paced. All depends on your curiosity, time and dedication. As instructors, we are here to transfer knowledge and experience the best we can.

And the community will be there for you to help with basic and advanced questions so we can all grow together! :)

Learn More

Is this course for me?

Are you an IT professional aiming to be a Cloud Engineer?
All the content is introductory but it will help you to have the basis for more advanced scenarios and tools.
Can you attend all classes, do exercises and offer help?
If you are attending the live classes with instructors, we recommend you to participate only if you can attend all classes and complete the exercises. Offer help to colleagues and ask for help anytime you need in one of our channels (in person, slack, GitHub PR, etc).
Are you willing to spend out of business hours to study?
This course will introduce a number of new concepts on a high level (level 100 and 200) in class and we expect students to achieve this same level or higher (level 300/400) by themselves, doing laboratories at home and participating in discussions.
Are you keen on learning new concepts and tools?
What will drive the transformation here is your own wish to learn and practice the concepts. We are just helping with a little push.
Do you have a laptop to follow the course in class?
Although you can use any operating system to follow the course, to quick catch up with other students in class we recommend using a Unix/Linux based OS. At this stage we will not provide support for Windows users.
Do you want to help other students and help other professionals?
As every community, collaboration is key so share what you have learned and keep a positive and proactive attitude. You absorb more what you learned when you teach someone.
